The Canopy: Three Fundamental Positions

We've established what you see the society around you as, now we must determine what you believe society should be. There are three positions one can take regarding the current hegemony and its resulting hierarchies:

  • Reformist: Support the preservation of the current state of affairs, looking to ensure the longevity of both the hegemony and current hierarchy through minor adaptations and reforms.
  • Reactionary: Support the seizing of current state of affairs, looking to take control of it to readjust the hierarchies towards what you believe more inherently suits the hegemony.
  • Radical: Support the abolition of current state of affairs, looking to do away with not just the current hierarchy, but also the underlying hegemony that creates it.

Of course this brings into question what exactly the hegemony centers itself around. The fundamental goal of society is to resolve conflict between humans, and as a result, it must be able to explain the differences between people both in who they are and the conditions they face.

  • Value: The concept of a universally translatable and quantifiable "worth" of a person or object, whether it be measured along civic, cultural, or material lines.
    • Value (according to the cultural line): The "value" of a person in regards to a proper society is judged by how they measure up to certain set of ethics/ideals
    • Value (according to the civic line) The "value" of a person in regards to a proper society is judged by their power/influence and usage of such)
    • Value (according to the material line) : The "value" of a person in regards to a proper society is judged by their control/ownership of resources
